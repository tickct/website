Local History

The Scroll and Triangle Club was formally organized in October 12, 1921 by C.W Carlson, D. Goedhart, Robert S. Mayo, Benjamin F. Morrison, H.W Munday, and L.T Smith. These men felt that the ideals of fellowship which they sought could be more perfectly realized and congeniality and genuine friendship made more essential by forming a local fraternity. The group was installed as the Armour Chapter of Triangle Fraternity in 1923. The Armour Chapter was the first to receive Rattle I from the Iowa Chapter. In 1940 the Armour Institute of Technology merged with Lewis University, a university located on Chicago’s Westside. The two universities consolidated on Armour’s Southside campus and changed the institution’s name to the Illinois Institute of Technology. Despite the university’s name change the Armour Chapter has kept its original founding name in homage to the chapter’s rich history.

Mission

All Triangle Fraternity brothers strive to be leaders in the campus, local community, and their chosen fields of studies. In order to do so our brothers and volunteers are aware of our mission statement and objectives. Triangle’s purpose is as follows:
 
The purpose of Triangle shall be to maintain a fraternity of engineers, architects and scientists. It shall carry out its purpose by establishing chapters that develop balanced men who cultivate high moral character, foster lifelong friendships, and live their lives with integrity.

Code of Ethics

All Triangle brothers hold themselves to a high moral standards and strive to be the best men they can in order build a better world for tomorrow. Triangle’s Code of Ethics reflects our fraternity’s beliefs and values.

Code of Ethics

As a member of Triangle, I recognize my obligation to:

  • Observe the precepts of the Fraternity as set forth in the Ritual;
  • Accept cheerfully my full share of any task, however menial, involved in maintaining a chapter home;
  • Preserve and promote the chosen ideals of my Fraternity;
  • Pay all personal bills promptly, and always live within my means;
  • Help create in my chapter home an environment in which enduring friendships may be formed;
  • Maintain a creditable scholastic record;
  • Promote the welfare of my profession;
  • Maintain my self-respect by proper conduct at all times;
  • Uphold faithfully the traditions and program of my Alma Mater;
  • Pay the price of success in honest effort.
